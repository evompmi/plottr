statistical cross-validation vs R 4.5.3 (2026-03-11)

  • Plöttr reruns every function in tools/stats.js against its R 4.5.3 counterpart on real built-in datasets (iris, PlantGrowth, ToothGrowth, mtcars, chickwts, InsectSprays, sleep, women, trees, airquality, warpbreaks).
  • Inputs are bit-identical between R and Plöttr.
  • Tolerance: |Δ| ≤ 0.005 on test statistics and on p-values ≥ 0.01. Deep-tail p-values (< 0.01) are compared in log space, so the ratio between R's p and Plöttr's stays within [1/1.1, 1.1].
  • Post-hoc tests (Games-Howell, Dunn-BH) are validated against PMCMRplus, the canonical R package for non-parametric multiple comparisons.
  • R-floor rows (amber): R's ptukey saturates at ~2.2e-15 due to a 1 − ptukey(q) cancellation. Plöttr's ptukey_upper computes the survival directly and continues the true tail past that floor (cross-checked against scipy and Monte Carlo). These rows are not JS failures — R is simply no longer ground truth there.
  • Real failures are flagged in red and counted honestly.
  • Reproduce locally: Rscript benchmark/run-r.R && node benchmark/run.js
